Convolutional Neural Network Classification of ASL Fingerspelling

Jack Miller, Jerry Yang, Aidan Shea, Shane Fitzpatrick, Joseph Fallait, and Sean Curley

American Sign Language is one of many sign languages used predominantly by individuals who are hard of hearing. More specifically, fingerspelling is the act of spelling out individual letters majorly in order to spell out words the user might not know. CNN Classification of American Sign language is a burgeoning field in the current landscape of Machine Learning image classification, and is being used to make education more accessible for deaf students in the face of the COVID pandemic. This project was completed as part of Boston College's undergraduate Machine Learning course, with the end-product being a convolutional neural network trained to classify images of fingerspelled letters.
